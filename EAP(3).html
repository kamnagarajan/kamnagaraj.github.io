Dr. J. J. Moran's account

Dr. John Joseph Moran, who was Poe's attending physician, gives a detailed account of Poe's appearance that day: “a stained faded, old bombazine coat, pantaloons of a similar character, a pair of worn-out shoes run down at the heels, and an old straw hat.” He also described his physical condition, “he had not the slightest odor of liquor upon his breath or person” and that during a period of consciousness, Poe held "vacant converse with spectral and imaginary objects on the walls. His face was pale and his whole person drenched in perspiration."
